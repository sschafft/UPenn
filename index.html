Community Sites

Open Source Social Network

Discourse is a cutting-edge open source community collaboration tool. It was recently developed in Februrary 2013 by several of the developers from Stack Exchange, a major online community. It features live-updating, authentication from a variety of sources (Google, Facebook, Twitter), email integration, threaded discussions and automatic rating and point systems. It provides a simple interface and allows for converations to be moderated easily.

It does not, however, provide support for events, projects or similar additional aspects that some communities might want. It simply focuses on maintaining and managing conversations. Given it is an open source tool, it may be modified as needed and hosted in any manner that you may need (on-premise, on the cloud or some combination of the two).

Enterprise Social Network

SocialCast is a robust enterprise focused online community tool. It allows you to host the tool on the cloud and use the tool as the traditional "Software as a Service", but it also allows you to have your a copy hosted on your own servers or on your own private Amazon Web Services server. This element of security can be very important for large organizations that may have privacy restrictions.

The tool features a streamlined interface and allows for easy integration into other enterprise tools or internal websites so that conversations can take place in context across the organization. It also privdes analaytics around community engagement. It, however, does come with a cost per user once your community goes above 50 users. It is also a closed-source application which means that you cannot make any changes to the actual application and can only interact with the tool through its API.

Public Social Network

Google+ communities is a free tool that is offered by Google that allows users to create groups (both private and public) that allows for discussions around key topics of interest. The tool integrates easily with other google tools (Google calendar and Gmail specifically). It allows for event creation and tracking in addition to general conversations. It also has very well executed video and photo sharing features.

Google communities, however, does not allow you to make any changes to the code base and you cannot host Google+ communites on your own servers. It does not integrate with many enterprise tools and requires you to have a Google account to use.

Additional Resources

The following is a collection of a variety of interesting resources, talks, and articles centered around online community development and social media strategy. This is by no means an exhaustive list and please feel free to sugguest any additions you think would be helpful by adding to the discussion at the end of this page.

Clay Shirky: Love, internet style (Video)

Noted Internet thinker and author Clay Shirky delivered one of the opening "provocations" at Supernova 2007. Using a 1300-year-old Japanese shrine as a metaphor, Clay explained how the New Network changes the basic dynamics of business and collective creativity.

Mashable: Tips on online community building

Sarah Kessler, currently an associate editor at FastCompany and previously at Mashable, shares some interesting insights and thoughts on how to develop a "world class online community".

Wikipedia's strategic plan development process & outcome (Video)

In 2009 Wikipedia, one of the largest online communities, began to undertake an interesting project in community based strategic plan devleopment. It involved a large quantity of its global community and developed a compelling strategic plan through 2015.

Additional links about Wikipedia's Strategy:

Harvard Business Review writes about Wikipedia's strategy PDF of Wikipedia's strategic plan document

Michael Silverman: 6 common mistakes of creating an online community

Michael Silverman, CEO of Duo Consulting and author of "Capturing Community: How To Build, Manage, and Market Your Online Community", shares some interseting insights around some common pitfalls that people can face when developing online communities.

McKinsey: How social intelligence can guide decisions

McKinsey discusses how social intelligence and being able to leverage social networks and online communities can provide insights that guide decision making.

Fast Company: 7 things your social media consultant should tell you

Anjali Mullany from Fast Company sheds some light on key areas to consider when looking at building your community and the role your internal or external social media consultant should play.

SkillSoft: 8 truths of social learning

Pam Boiros, Corporate Marketing VP at Skillsoft, discusses the fundamental requirements for a successful social learning program and provides some valuable advice to consider.

Jeff Attwood: Forums are Dead, Long Live Forums

Jeff Attwood, co-founder of Stack Exchange and recent co-founder of Discourse, discusses changes in the world of online forums. Forums are some of the most long-lived forms for building online communities, usually around specific content areas.

Shuling Liao & En-yi Chou: Intention to adopt knowledge through virtual communities: posters vs lurkers

Shuling Liao and En-yi Chou recently published a study that "aims to examine the social capital and technical determinants of knowledge adoption intentions in virtual communities and to explore the differences between posters and lurkers."
